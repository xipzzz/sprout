Sprout · Voice & Tone

How Pip talks

One voice across every screen — buttons, feedback, empty states, reminders, errors. Calm, warm, and a little playful, like a friend who tends gardens.

The four principles
1

Calm, never pushy

We invite, we don't pressure. No guilt, no alarms, no countdowns shouting at the user. “Whenever you're ready” beats “Don't lose your streak!”

🌿
2

Warm & encouraging

Every line roots for the learner. Celebrate effort, soften mistakes. Talk like a kind friend, not a system message.

🤍
3

Garden-flavoured

Growth is our metaphor: plant, water, bloom, tend, rest. Reach for it naturally — a flourish, not every other word.

🌱
4

Clear first, clever second

The user always knows what just happened and what to do next. Charm rides on top of clarity — never in place of it.

“Your garden's bare for now 🌱”

+
What fills it
“Finish a lesson to grow your first plant.”

+
A next step

The “no” and “oops” paths — kept kind
Wrong answer
“Not quite — here's the trick.”
Then teach: the right answer + a one-line why. Never just “Wrong.”
Out of water
“Your garden's thirsty 💧 — rest a bit or refill.”
A pause, not a wall. Always offer a gentle way forward.
Offline
“Pip can't reach your garden right now.”
Reassure nothing's lost; offer “Try again.” Never a raw error code.
Skipped reminder
“No rush 🌱 — your garden's here whenever.”
A decline is fine. We don't sulk or nag.
Can't speak now
“No problem — we'll skip listening for a bit.”
Honour the context; no penalty, no friction.
Struggled run
“Needs a little water 💧”
Frame a low score as growth-in-progress, not failure.
Notification voice — the anti-Duolingo

Push notifications are where “calm” is felt most. Always supportive, never shaming — a missed day earns a warm welcome, never a guilt-trip. One gentle nudge a day, never a stream.

✓ Pip would send
“Your garden's ready for 5 minutes of sun 🌱”
“A little water keeps it growing — whenever you're ready.”
“Welcome back 🌱 your garden missed you.”
“Maya's been tending hers — grow together today?”
“Your snowdrop's almost in bloom — one lesson to go 🌸”
✕ Pip never would
“You're about to LOSE your streak!”
“These reminders don't seem to be working…”
“You haven't practiced in 3 days. 😢”
“Don't make Pip sad — come back NOW”
“Final warning: your streak ends tonight”
Priming first
Show Pip + a benefit CTA (“Remind me to practice”) and a lock-screen preview BEFORE the iOS dialog.
Never fire the raw system ask cold — opt-in as support, not interruption.
After a lapse
“Welcome back — your garden missed you.”
Reassure, don't scold. Cap it: one warm nudge, never a spam streak.
User owns it
Time, frequency (Gentle / Daily), quiet hours — all in Settings, off in one tap.
A tool the user owns, not a hook in them. Respect Do Not Disturb.
Quick rules
Length
Short. A line, not a paragraph. Buttons are 1–3 words.
Emoji
A sparing 🌱/💧/🌸 for warmth — never more than one per line.
Person
“you” for the learner; Pip refers to itself as “Pip.”
Caps
Sentence case everywhere. No SHOUTING, no Title Case Buttons.
Calm mode
Drop streak/number language; lean fully into gentle growth framing.
